Open-source engine

What stays public in FinStack MCP.

This repo stays public. It is the engine, the credibility layer, and the top of the funnel. It should win on usefulness and clarity, not on hype.

Public does not mean shallow. The job of this page is to show exactly what exists, how it installs, why Indian-market users should care, and how the future paid layer fits on top without pretending the hosted stack is already finished.

What the OSS repo does

  • NSE and BSE quotes, indices, corporate actions, quarterly results
  • Global stocks, crypto, forex, SEC filings, and fundamentals
  • Technical indicators, stock comparison, sector performance, options chain
  • Reusable data engine for future email, Telegram, and dashboard products

What the OSS repo does not try to be

  • Not the whole SaaS business on its own
  • Not a generic "finance MCP but with more tools" story
  • Not the place for billing, private customer logic, or closed dashboards
  • Not dependent on large marketing spend to be useful
Paid wedge

The paid product is the Indian market daily brief.

This is the fastest bridge from the current repo to revenue. It is easier to ship, easier to explain, and easier to repeat daily than a broad portfolio platform or a sales-heavy advisor suite.

The brief is meant for people who care about usable output, not raw data dumps: morning market context, watchlist triggers, earnings dates, corporate actions, and a compact written summary delivered daily.

Step 01 Collect market state

Indices, movers, sector performance, FII/DII context, bulk deals, and watchlist events.

Step 02 Generate the brief

The daily brief engine now produces structured JSON plus readable plain-text, Telegram, and email-ready output.

Step 03 Format for delivery

Turn the brief into email, Telegram, WhatsApp, or dashboard output depending on the user tier.

Step 04 Sell recurring value

Charge for daily watchlist intelligence and creator or desk workflows instead of generic access alone.
